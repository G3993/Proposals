I. Why

Thank you for Sunday night.

We had a blast co-creating with you, and are thrilled by the opportunity to bring ETHEREA permanently into your beautiful home.

ETHEREA's Imagination Engine is a dynamic, always-evolving creative intelligence that gives anyone the ability to instantly use their words to project their imagination in the space around them. We continue to discover new use cases and audiences for this technology — musical performances, animated poetry, immersive audiovisual theatre — and believe that your prolific hosting will provide a living laboratory to demonstrate its immense potential while entertaining and inspiring your guests.

Your home becomes more than a space where people gather. It becomes the space where people experience what's next — where a dinner party becomes an act of collective imagination, and where every guest leaves having seen something they've never seen before.

II. Who

Your guests. Your world.

ETHEREA is built for the room you already fill. The dinner guests, the artists, the thinkers, the friends who stay late. It doesn't require technical skill or setup — anyone can walk up and speak, and the space responds.

For casual evenings, ETHEREA runs itself — generating ambient, evolving visuals that set a mood without demanding attention. For performances and gatherings, it becomes an instrument that your guests play with their voices and presence. For one-on-one moments, the Imagination Guide becomes a conversational companion that can take you anywhere.

The system adapts to whoever is in the room. A child discovers it differently than a poet. A musician uses it differently than a comedian. That range is the point.

III. How

Making it happen.

The Compute Question

After some thought, Gonzo and I have concluded that installing a B300 inside an apartment is infeasible. They are only sold in nodes of eight which are soldered together, with cooling and power requirements that exceed residential capacity.

We are exploring what it might take to gain access to frontier compute inside the NYC metro area — necessary for reducing latency to power live performances at the highest level of production and reliability. We are exploring the feasibility of building a small cooperative data center inside of the 20,000 square foot tech/art accelerator we are building in North Williamsburg.

In the meantime, we can get started immediately with our remote provider, supported by NVIDIA's current top of the line professional workstation living in your home. This has the added benefit of keeping us flexible as the model landscape shifts.

Spatial Canvas

We will install a combination of ultra short throw projectors, microphones, and cameras throughout your home to enable multi-screen visibility and interactivity at day and night, all centrally controlled via an iOS app.

Local Brain

An RTX PRO 6000 workstation powers everything latency-sensitive: real-time transcription, audioreactivity, a local language model, and a smaller local version of ETHEREA that will become more capable over time. The workstation is modular and upgradeable.

As discussed, the workstation can be housed in a commissioned glass-blown aquarium that water-cools the GPU while refracting and magnifying the system's generative visuals through water and glass — a living sculpture that is simultaneously art object, light source, and the brain of the entire installation. We work with a very talented glass sculptor, Edoardo Cozzani, who would be a fantastic choice to bring this to life.

Cloud Compute

A B300 currently runs about $5–6/hr, making 24/7 operation around $4,500/mo. The cloud handles the heavy generative workloads — frontier model inference, image and video synthesis — while the local workstation handles everything real-time. As GPU supply increases, we expect these costs to decrease.
